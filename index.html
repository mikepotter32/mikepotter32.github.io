I'm going to take some time and write about CyanogenMod and the great developers behind this project. Mainly Steve Kondik.

CyanogenMod is developed as FOSS based on the official releases of Android by Google. Steve Kondik is the founder of this project. In September of 2008 enthusiasts found a way to attain root access on the HTC Dream phone. Having this root access and the open nature of android in general led to the phone’s stock firmware being modified and re-installed. Eventually certain firmware became popular with Steve Kondik’s Cyanogen leading the charge. Since Cyanogen is free and open source many developers gravitated towards it, within months, the number of devices and features supported by CyanogenMod blossomed.

Today CyanogenMod has recorded over 12 million active installs on a multitude of devices. Although not every device is supported, Cyanogen is a nice way to receive updates and some new features of android on older phones, where the OEM may have cut off support.

Steve Kondik actively contributes too many repositories on GitHub, specifically many firmware updates for newer devices, such as the OnePlus One, Oppo, etc. Steve has also created many repositories – The CM kernel, many Samsung Galaxy kernels, and the cm-updater-manifest.

CyanogenMod, I find very interesting, with the attempts from google trying to shut Cyanogen down, to the increased popularity of the software. It’s a custom ROM with no bloatware, root enabled, it receives updates quicker and for the most part offers better performance than most OEM roms.

Steve’s brand can be seen all over the internet, @Cyanogen. Twitter, GitHub, he goes by his real name on Facebook, and google+.

Even the metro apps are being updated to work in window panes like everything else in windows. Integrating the live tiles into the start menu makes sense, and improves functionality.
